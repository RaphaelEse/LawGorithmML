         No Overseas Congressional Help for International Negotiations with Adversaries Act NO CHINA Act
      
   
   
      II
      118th CONGRESS
      1st Session
      S. 3009
      IN THE SENATE OF THE UNITED STATES
      
         October 4, 2023
         
            Mr. Scott of Florida introduced the following bill; which was read twice and referred to the Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs
      
      A BILL
      To prohibit the use of Federal funds and resources for congressional travel to the People's Republic of China.
   
   
      Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,
      
         1.
         SHORT TITLE
         This Act may be cited as the 
               No Overseas Congressional Help for International Negotiations with Adversaries Act
             or the 
               NO CHINA Act
            .
      
      
         2.
         PROHIBITION OF FEDERALLY FUNDED CONGRESSIONAL TRAVEL TO CHINA
         
            (a)
            In general
            Notwithstanding any other provision of lawâ€”
            
               (1)
               Federal funds made available before, on, or after the date of enactment of this Act shall not be expended for any congressional delegation or other congressional trip to the People's Republic of China; and
            
            
               (2)
               Federal resources, such as military aircraft, shall not be used for any such delegation or trip. 
            
         
         
            (b)
            Rule of construction
            Nothing in this section shall be construed to apply to congressional travel to Taiwan.
